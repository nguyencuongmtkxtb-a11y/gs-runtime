# 5-Dimensional Design Critique

After producing ANY design artifact, perform a self-critique on these 5 dimensions. Score each 1-10 with a specific reason.

## Scoring Rubric

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1-3 | Fundamental flaws — needs rework |
| 4-6 | Adequate — meets basic requirements |
| 7-8 | Good — above average, few issues |
| 9-10 | Excellent — production-ready, polished |

## Dimensions

### 1. Philosophy (Design Philosophy Coherence)
Does the design have a clear, consistent philosophy? Does it align with the brand's identity and the design system's principles?

**Checklist:**
- Clear visual point of view
- Consistent mood/atmosphere
- Brand alignment
- No conflicting visual languages

Are the details polished? Is typography precise and spacing consistent throughout?

**Checklist:**
- Consistent spacing (no drifting margins/padding)
- Typography uses design system font stack
- Proper line heights and letter-spacing
- No orphaned elements or misalignments

## Acceptance Criteria

**Total Score: ___ / 50**

- **>= 40**: Excellent — ready to ship
- **35-39**: Good — minor improvements needed
- **30-34**: Needs work — identify specific improvements
- **< 30**: Must rework — fundamental issues

## Improvement Plan (if < 35)
1. Identify the lowest-scoring dimension
2. List 3 specific actions to improve it
3. Re-critique after changes
